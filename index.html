We aim to build biomedical devices for drug testing on different biological cells subjected to specific disease conditions. Specifically, the technology we will base our devices on consists of microelectromechanical systems (MEMS) supported on conducting polymers. The devices can be used for testing the action of parasites, viruses and drugs on bioelectric cells like cardiac cells and neurons and quantify the effect that the pathological environment creates on their electro-mechanical signaling on real time and with very high resolution. On the video above we show the action of one of our prototypes stimulating a cardiac cell. The setup configuration is shown on the left, and the results of the measurements produced by this system are shown below.

On this crowfunding site we seek to obtaing seed funding to achieve two very important technological milestones that will give us total novelty and advantage in the big biomedical devices market:

  1. Miniaturize all the setup above by embedding in one sleek and convenient case all the electronic equipment used to record and pre-process the device's signals.
  2. Connect wirelessly with smart devices (PC, tablets, smart phones, etc) to exchange data with backend servers for real-time, centralized processing and analysis using cutting-edge algorithms developed by the biomedical research community.

FAQ

Who are this devices aimed at?
The devices can be sold to research laboratories, pharmaceutical companies, universities, government labs, research hospital, etc. that perform advanced research on this kind of cellular systems. We also envision the development of advanced research trials on animals or clinical models where the devices critical role in discovering disease action mechanisms. Additionally, portable versions can be developed to provide lab-on-a-chip capabilities for on-field applications.
